Publications from the Fate of the Caribou Project

  Critical summer foraging tradeoffs in a subarctic ungulate Scientific paper An analysis of the feeding behavior of caribou using video camera collers, by Ehlers et al. 2021
Are migrating caribou losing the collective memory they rely on to survive? Article Article by Anne Gunn and Don Russell on caribou population collapse and threats to generational memory, in issue 1.2022 of the World Wildlife Fund's "The Circle" magezine (article begins on page 24)
Mapping tundra ecosystem plant functional type cover, height and aboveground biomass in Alaska and northwest Canada using unmanned aerial vehicles Scientific paper A study using remote sensing to map vegetation changes in the Arctic, by Katie Orndahl et. al 2022
Complimentary methods for identifying calving grounds of barren-ground caribou Scientific poster A summary of development of new methods to map caribou calving grounds, by Couriot et al. Presented at the 2022 joint meeting of the Ecological Society of America and the Canadian Society for Ecology and Evolution
Memories of migrations past: Sociality and congnition in dynamic, seasonal environments Scientific paper A study using computer modeling to examine the social habits of caribou over various space- and time-scales, by Gurarie et al. 2021
Update on the global status of wild reindeer and caribou Report A brief status update on global caribou & reindeer populations, prepared by Drs. Anne Gunn and Don Russell for the International Union for Conservation of Nature Deer Specialist Group
Optimism for Caribou Book chapter A review of caribou in the Canadian Arctic by Anne Gunn and others, in the book "Lines in the Snow" edited by Clive Tesar and P. Whitney Lackenbauer

Analysis tools from the Fate of the Caribou Project

  • lsatTS: An R package that enables users to extract nearly four decades of Landsat satellite surface reflectance measurements for sample locations anywhere on Earth and then generate high-quality time series of vegetation greenness for ecosystem monitoring, by Berner et al.

Other relevant resources

  • Northern Caribou Canada: A collaborative website curated by the Wekʼèezhìi Renewable Resources Board with extensive information, news updates, and maps of caribou herds in Canada.
  • Northwest Territories Research Bulletin: Summaries of recent research on caribou and other species in the Northwest Territories, Canada
  • Northwest Territories Discovery Portal: Data and reports from cumulative impacts monitoring in the Northwest Territories, Canada
  • The Great Caribou Migration: Storymap about the Western Arctic Herd (Alaska)
  • Canadian caribou status report: Assessment and status report of barren-ground caribou in Canada (2016)
  • CircumArctic Rangifer Monitoring and Assessment Network: General information and herd-specific statuses of arctic caribou
  • Influence of the Ekati Diamond Mine on migratory tundra caribou movements: a 2021 report on caribou movement around a large open-pit mine
  • 2023 North American Caribou Workshop & Arctic Ungulate Conference: Join Fate of the Caribou team members and other caribou researchers to share knowledge. May 8-12, 2023, Amchorage, Alaska.
  • Relationships between caribou and industrial infrustructure in NWT: Academic thesis on interactions between barren-ground caribou and the seasonal Tibbitt to Contwoyto mining road, by Angus Smith 2022.
  • Trends in range use by Bathurst caribou: Academic thesis on spatial and temporal trends in range use by Bathurst caribou during a population decline, 1997 - 2019, by Robin Mennell 2022.
  • Lines in the Snow: Thoughts on the past and future of Northern Canadian Policy Issues: A book edited by Clive Tesar and P. Whitney Lackenbauer with contributing authors providing context for many issues relevant to life and landuse in the Canadian Arctic
